## Answers

### 1. [Recall]
**Answer:** **Delay** — time to complete operation (often ms), e.g. RTT or request response time.

---

### 2. [Recall]
**Answer:** **Maximum capacity** of link; **Mbps or Gbps**.

---

### 3. [Recall]
**Answer:** **Bandwidth** = theoretical max; **throughput** = **actually achieved** rate (≤ bandwidth).

---

### 4. [Understanding]
**Answer:** **Unlikely/help minimally** — 2 KB transfer instant on any modern link; **250 ms** = latency/processing/DB/region, not office bandwidth.

---

### 5. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Each call waits **at least one RTT + server time** **in series**; times **add**, not parallelized.

---

### 6. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Serves from **nearby edge** — lower **RTT/latency** per file vs distant origin.

---

### 7. [Application]
**Answer:** 50 MB = 400 Mbit; 10 Mbps → **~40 seconds** order of magnitude (400/10).

---

### 8. [Recall]
**Answer:** **Round-Trip Time** — packet to destination and back.

---

### 9. [Analysis]
**Answer:** TCP **window/congestion** limits; **single-flow** cap; CPU/SSL overhead; distance/latency limiting BDP fill; not all links sustain theoretical max one stream.

---

### 10. [Understanding]
**Answer:** **Latency** — extra **RTT** (OPTIONS) before main request.

---

### 11. [Application]
**Answer:** **Throughput** delivered to video client (or latency/jitter to CDN); speedtest ≠ app path sustained rate.

---

### 12. [Understanding]
**Answer:** At least **~1 RTT** before application data after connect (handshake).

---

### 13. [Analysis]
**Answer:** **Latency** percentiles (p95/p99); **error rate**; **response size** / resource usage — RPS alone hides slow or failing requests.

---

### 14. [Application]
**Answer:** **Latency (RTT)** between API and DB on every query.

---

### 15. [Recall]
**Answer:** How much data can be **in flight** on wire (bandwidth × RTT); needed to saturate high bandwidth high latency links.
