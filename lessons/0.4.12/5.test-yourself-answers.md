## Answers

### 1. [Recall]
**Answer:** **Reachability** (if replies) and **RTT/latency** per packet.

---

### 2. [Recall]
**Answer:** **`tracert`**

---

### 3. [Recall]
**Answer:** **ICMP** Echo Request/Reply.

---

### 4. [Understanding]
**Answer:** **Inbound ICMP blocked** on server/firewall (`0.4.7`); **HTTP/TCP 443** still works. ping ≠ app health.

---

### 5. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Each increment reaches **next router**; TTL=0 triggers ICMP time exceeded → discover hop addresses.

---

### 6. [Understanding]
**Answer:** **Firewall/SG blocks 443**; service **not listening** on 443; wrong host. ICMP allowed separately from TCP.

---

### 7. [Application]
**Answer:** **DNS** — `nslookup`/`dig`/`Resolve-DnsName` hostname; fix name resolution before path diagnostics.

---

### 8. [Application]
**Answer:** **`Test-NetConnection hostname -Port 443`** (or `curl -v https://...`).

---

### 9. [Analysis]
**Answer:** **Not necessarily** — router may **not respond** to probes; traffic may still pass. Check later hops and end-to-end curl.

---

### 10. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Different **protocol** (ICMP/UDP vs TCP); **asymmetric routing**; load balancers may affect HTTP only.

---

### 11. [Application]
**Answer:** **Long-haul link** (geography) or peering change — aligns with latency lesson; consider regional deploy/CDN.

---

### 12. [Recall]
**Answer:** **`-c 8`** (Linux/macOS). Windows: **`-n 8`**.

---

### 13. [Analysis]
**Answer:** ping = **ICMP/IP** only; API needs **TCP port + HTTP** — use curl/health endpoint; auth/CORS separate layers.

---

### 14. [Understanding]
**Answer:** ping RTT ≈ **round-trip latency** at IP layer — subset of total request latency (`0.4.11`).

---

### 15. [Application]
**Answer:** Example: (1) **DNS** resolve name — nslookup; (2) **ping** IP/host for basic RTT/loss; (3) **curl** https URL or Test-NetConnection :443 for service. Order: name → IP path → app port.
