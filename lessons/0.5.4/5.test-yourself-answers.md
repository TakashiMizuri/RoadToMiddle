## Answers

### 1. [Recall]
**Answer:** `<ul>`.

### 2. [Recall]
**Answer:** `<dl>`, `<dt>`, `<dd>`.

### 3. [Recall]
**Answer:** `<li>` (list items).

### 4. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Когда порядок пунктов меняет смысл — steps, ranking, numbered instructions.
**Why:** ol conveys sequence semantics.

### 5. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Внутрь родительского `<li>`.
**Why:** Valid nesting model; AT hierarchy.

### 6. [Understanding]
**Answer:** dl pairs terms with definitions; ul is flat group without term-dd relationship.
**Why:** Different content models.

### 7. [Application]
**Answer:**
```html
<nav>
  <ul>
    <li><a href="/">Home</a></li>
    <li><a href="/docs">Docs</a></li>
    <li><a href="/contact">Contact</a></li>
  </ul>
</nav>
```

### 8. [Application]
**Answer:** Top level `<ol>` for steps; nested `<ul>` (or ol if sub-steps ordered) inside step 2's `<li>`.
**Why:** Sequence at top; sub-details under one step.

### 9. [Application]
**Answer:** `<dl><dt>HTTP</dt><dd>...</dd></dl>`.
**Why:** Term-definition pattern.

### 10. [Analysis]
**Answer:** B — anchor direct child of ul.
**Why:** Must wrap in li.

### 11. [Analysis]
**Answer:** Footer links order usually not meaningful — should be `ul`; ol misleads AT ("step 1 of 3").
**Why:** Choose list type by semantics.

### 12. [Understanding]
**Answer:** User knows count and can jump list navigation commands; structure exposed.
**Why:** List semantics in a11y API.
