# Exercises: DNS

> Complete after lectures and passing `4.test-yourself.md`.

## Easy

### E1. Record type picker
**Goal:** Know A vs AAAA vs CNAME.

Choose record type(s) for each goal:

1. Point `api.example.com` to load balancer IPv4
2. Enable IPv6 clients for same host
3. Point `www.example.com` to `example.com` hostname
4. Verify domain ownership for Let's Encrypt DNS-01

**Success criteria:** A, AAAA, CNAME, TXT respectively.

---

### E2. TTL math
**Goal:** Reason about cache windows.

Record had TTL=3600. Authoritative change at T=0. Worst case when last recursive cache still serves old value?

**Success criteria:** ~3600s after change for that cache entry (plus stub layers).

---

## Medium

### M1. Trace resolution
**Goal:** See recursive path.

Run `nslookup -debug example.com` or `dig +trace example.com`. List at least three servers involved (root/TLD/authoritative or your recursive).

**Success criteria:** Ordered delegation described.

---

### M2. hosts override
**Goal:** Link 0.4.6 preview.

Add test entry to hosts file mapping fake name to 127.0.0.1. `ping` or `curl` the name. Remove after. What breaks if you forget to remove before production deploy?

**Success criteria:** Explains local-only override.

---

## Hard (stretch)

### H1. Cutover plan
**Goal:** Production DNS migration.

Write 5-step plan to move `api.prod.com` A record from old LB to new LB with minimal stale traffic. Include TTL strategy and verification.

**Success criteria:** Pre-lower TTL, change, monitor, rollback mention.
