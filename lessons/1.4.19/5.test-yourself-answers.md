# Answers: Exception handling in async

## Scoring

≥80% → `1.4.20`

---

## Answers

### 1. [Recall]
**Answer:** **Inner exception** типа fault (e.g. **`InvalidOperationException`**), **не** **`AggregateException`**.

---

### 2. [Recall]
**Answer:** **`AggregateException`** (often with inner).

---

### 3. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Awaiter **`GetResult()`** designed to throw **direct** exception for await pattern; blocking API **`Wait()`** historically wraps multiple/task faults in **`AggregateException`**.

---

### 4. [Understanding]
**Answer:** **One** catch execution; typically **one** exception (**first** fault) — second on **`t2.Exception`**.

---

### 5. [Application]
**Answer:** After **`WhenAll`**, iterate **`tasks.Where(t => t.IsFaulted)`** and read **`t.Exception`**, or catch and rethrow **`new AggregateException(...)`**.

---

### 6. [Application]
**Answer:** **Swallows error** — caller thinks success; log and rethrow or return error result.

---

### 7. [Analysis]
**Answer:** **`throw;`** preserves stack; **`throw ex;`** **resets stack trace** — hides async frames.

---

### 8. [Analysis]
**Answer:** **Unhandled on SyncContext** — can **crash app** / unobservable by caller — **`1.4.13`**.

---

### 9. [Understanding]
**Answer:** **Да** — **`finally`** runs on fault path before exception propagates (unless fatal abort).

---

### 10. [Recall]
**Answer:** Exception on **faulted task never awaited/observed** — finalizer path **`TaskScheduler.UnobservedTaskException`**.

---

### 11. [Application]
**Answer:** **Да** — filter evaluated when exception reaches **`catch`**.

---

### 12. [Understanding]
**Answer:** When exception **propagates unhandled** out of action delegate — after async state machine completes fault.

---

### 13. [Analysis]
**Answer:** **`AggregateException`** + **sync-over-async deadlock/starvation** (**`1.4.14`**, **`1.4.20`**).

---

### 14. [Application]
**Answer:** **Rethrow with original stack** when marshaling across threads without await.

---

### 15. [Understanding]
**Answer:** Often catch **`OperationCanceledException`** (base) for cooperative cancel; distinguish unexpected **`TaskCanceledException`** from timeouts if needed.

---
