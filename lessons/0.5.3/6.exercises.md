# Exercises: Headings and Text Semantics

> Complete after lectures and passing `4.test-yourself.md`. Ask AI for hints, not full solutions.

## Easy

### E1. Fix heading order
**Goal:** Correct outline without CSS.

Broken snippet:

```html
<h1>Company Handbook</h1>
<h3>Vacation Policy</h3>
<h4>Request Process</h4>
```

Rewrite with proper levels only (h1–h3). Do not add CSS.

**Success criteria:** h1 → h2 → h3 chain; same text preserved.

---

### E2. Paragraphs vs br
**Goal:** Semantic paragraphs.

Convert to three `<p>` elements (remove br soup):

```html
<p>First idea.<br><br>Second idea.<br><br>Third idea.</p>
```

**Success criteria:** Three separate p tags; no br between paragraphs.

---

## Medium

### M1. Mini article
**Goal:** Combine headings + text semantics.

Create `guide.html` (with skeleton from `0.5.1`) inside `<main>`:
- One h1: "Local Setup Guide"
- Two sections (use `<section>` with h2): "Prerequisites" and "Install"
- Each section: two `<p>`; in Install, one `<strong>` warning and one `<em>` stressed word
- One `<mark>` around a CLI keyword users search for

**Success criteria:** Valid hierarchy; strong/em/mark used with meaning.

---

### M2. strong vs em judgment
**Goal:** Pick correct tag.

For each phrase, write **strong**, **em**, or **neither** (plain text):

1. "This is **urgent**: patch today."
2. "I **did** tell you yesterday." (contradiction stress)
3. Product name styled bold in marketing with no extra importance

**Success criteria:** 1 strong, 2 em, 3 neither or b with justification.
